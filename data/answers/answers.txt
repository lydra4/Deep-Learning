Does Robb Stark Die? - Yes, Robb Stark dies in George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series. He is killed during the Red Wedding, which takes place in the third book, "A Storm of Swords." The Red Wedding is a massacre orchestrated by the Freys and the Boltons, where Robb, along with his mother Catelyn and many of his bannermen, are betrayed and murdered..

Does Ned Stark die? - Yes, Ned Stark dies. He is executed in the first book of the "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, "A Game of Thrones," by George R.R. Martin. His death is ordered by King Joffrey Baratheon, despite previous assurances that he would be spared..
Did Renly Baratheon claim the Iron Throne? - Yes, Renly Baratheon did claim the Iron Throne. In George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, Renly Baratheon, the younger brother of King Robert Baratheon, declares himself king and claims the Iron Throne after Robert's death. He gathers support from many of the southern lords and forms a large army, believing that he has a stronger claim to the throne than his nephew, Joffrey Baratheon. This is because Renly argues that Joffrey is not the legitimate son of Robert but rather the product of an incestuous relationship between Queen Cersei Lannister and her brother Jaime Lannister..
Does Sansa Stark marry Tyrion Lannister? - Yes, Sansa Stark does marry Tyrion Lannister in George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series. Their marriage is arranged by Tywin Lannister, Tyrion's father, to solidify Lannister control over the North. However, the marriage is never consummated, and Sansa eventually escapes from King's Landing, leaving the marriage effectively null..
Was Lysa Arryn pushed through the Moon Door? - Yes, Lysa Arryn was pushed through the Moon Door. In George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, specifically in "A Storm of Swords," Lysa Arryn is pushed through the Moon Door by Petyr Baelish, also known as Littlefinger, at the Eyrie..
Does Bronn fight for Tyrion in his trial by combat at the Eyrie? - Yes, Bronn fights for Tyrion in his trial by combat at the Eyrie. In "A Game of Thrones," the first book of the "A Song of Ice and Fire" series by George R.R. Martin, Tyrion Lannister demands a trial by combat after being accused of attempting to murder Bran Stark. Bronn, a sellsword who has befriended Tyrion, agrees to be his champion and wins the trial by combat against Ser Vardis Egen, securing Tyrion's freedom..
Is Robert Baratheon the father of Joffrey Baratheon? - In the "A Song of Ice and Fire" series by George R.R. Martin, Robert Baratheon is believed to be the father of Joffrey Baratheon. However, it is later revealed that Joffrey's biological father is actually Jaime Lannister, who is the twin brother of Robert's wife, Cersei Lannister. This revelation is a significant plot point in the series, as it challenges the legitimacy of Joffrey's claim to the Iron Throne..
Does Sandor Clegane have a fear of fire? - Yes, Sandor Clegane, also known as "The Hound," has a fear of fire. This fear stems from a traumatic incident in his childhood when his brother, Gregor Clegane, pushed his face into a brazier, causing severe burns and leaving him with a lasting phobia of fire. This fear is a significant aspect of his character in "A Song of Ice and Fire" series by George R.R. Martin..
Does Davos Seaworth serve Stannis Baratheon? - Yes, Davos Seaworth serves Stannis Baratheon. In George R. R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, Davos is a loyal supporter and trusted advisor to Stannis Baratheon.
