Does Robb Stark Die? - Yes, Robb Stark dies in the "A Song of Ice and Fire" series by George R.R. Martin. He is killed during the event known as the Red Wedding, which takes place in the third book, "A Storm of Swords." During this event, Robb, his mother Catelyn Stark, and many of his bannermen are betrayed and murdered at the wedding feast of Edmure Tully and Roslin Frey..
Which hand does Jamie Lannister lose? - Jaime Lannister loses his right hand..
Does Ned Stark die? - Yes, Ned Stark dies. In George R.R. Martin's "A Game of Thrones," which is the first book in the "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, Ned Stark is executed. His death occurs towards the end of the book, ordered by King Joffrey Baratheon, despite initial promises that he would be spared if he confessed to treason..
Did Renly Baratheon claim the Iron Throne? - Yes, Renly Baratheon did claim the Iron Throne. In George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, Renly Baratheon, the youngest brother of King Robert Baratheon, declared himself king following Robert's death. He based his claim on his popularity, his marriage to Margaery Tyrell, and the support of the powerful House Tyrell and its bannermen. Renly's claim was contested by his older brother, Stannis Baratheon, who believed he had the rightful claim under the Baratheon line of succession..
Does Sansa Stark marry Tyrion Lannister? - Yes, Sansa Stark does marry Tyrion Lannister in George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series. Their marriage is arranged by Tywin Lannister, Tyrion's father, as part of his political strategy to gain control over the North. However, the marriage is never consummated, and Sansa eventually escapes King's Landing..
Was Lysa Arryn pushed through the Moon Door? - Yes, Lysa Arryn was pushed through the Moon Door. In George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, specifically in "A Storm of Swords," Lysa Arryn is pushed through the Moon Door by Petyr Baelish, also known as Littlefinger..
Does Bronn fight for Tyrion in his trial by combat at the Eyrie? - Yes, Bronn fights for Tyrion Lannister in his trial by combat at the Eyrie. In "A Game of Thrones," the first book of George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, Tyrion demands a trial by combat to prove his innocence after being accused of attempting to murder Bran Stark. Bronn, a skilled sellsword, volunteers to fight on Tyrion's behalf and defeats Ser Vardis Egen, winning the trial and securing Tyrion's release..
Is Robert Baratheon the father of Joffrey Baratheon? - No, Robert Baratheon is not the biological father of Joffrey Baratheon. Joffrey is actually the son of Jaime Lannister and Cersei Lannister, who are siblings. This secret parentage is a central plot point in George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series, leading to significant political and personal conflicts throughout the story..
Does Sandor Clegane have a fear of fire? - Yes, Sandor Clegane, also known as "The Hound," has a fear of fire. This fear is rooted in a traumatic childhood incident where his brother, Gregor Clegane, pushed his face into a brazier, causing severe burns and leaving him scarred. This event left Sandor with a deep-seated fear of fire throughout his life..
Does Davos Seaworth serve Stannis Baratheon? - Yes, Davos Seaworth serves Stannis Baratheon. Davos is a loyal supporter of Stannis and serves as his trusted advisor. He is often referred to as the "Onion Knight" and is a prominent character in George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" series..
